# 2026 Masters Pool Strategy

## The Problem: Chalk-Heavy Fields

In the 2023 pool (14 entries, 8 picks each):
- Scheffler appeared on 86% of entries
- McIlroy on 79%, Rahm 64%, Conners 64%
- Top 7 players covered 50%+ of entries
- Only 25 unique players across 112 total picks

When everyone picks the same favorites, the pool becomes a coin flip on 1-2 differentiator picks. Shared winners don't separate you from the field.

## Contrarian Strategy

The pool pays total prize money, not score vs par. This means:
1. A player finishing T10 alone ($630K) is worth more than a player in a 5-way T5 split
2. You don't need your longshot to win — a top-20 finish from a player nobody else has is a massive edge
3. The downside of a missed pick is capped (they just earn $0 if cut), but the upside of a unique hit is uncapped

### Approach
- **Identify consensus picks** — players everyone will take (top 5-10 in betting odds)
- **Take 1-2 chalk max** — enough to stay competitive if favorites dominate
- **Fill remaining 4-5 slots with high-upside, low-ownership players** — guys with real Augusta credentials or hot recent form that the casual pool entrant won't think of
- **Target the T10–T25 range** — players with realistic top-20 equity that won't be heavily owned

### Key Metrics
- **Implied win probability** — from betting odds, indicates overall strength
- **Projected ownership** — how many other entries will have this player
- **Leverage** = upside probability / projected ownership — high leverage = underowned relative to talent
- **Augusta history** — past Masters finishes matter more here than any other major
